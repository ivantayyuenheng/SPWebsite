Should We Save The Earth?

Yes! WE SHOULD!

The Earth is approaching its breaking point. Humans have harmed many lands and waters, causing ecosystem concerns such as global warming and climate change. Earth is our only source of living and staying places where life is achievable.

Not only that, but Earth is the only planet that provides us with food and water to sustain life, and we must take responsibility for caring for our own planet. Furthermore, rather than destroying nature's ecosystem, we must ensure that animals have habitats in which to live.

Overall, Earth is our planet, and we must accept the duty of ensuring that we take proper care of it.

How to Save The Earth?

1. Reduce, Reuse, Recycle.

REUSE THE PAST, RECYCLE THE PRESENT, SAVE THE FUTURE

Instead of throwing away waste causing pile of garbage surrounding our Earth. On the contrary, we should follow the three "R's" to reduce landfill area and make our planet a cleaner environment.

By reducing, we reduce the trash produced in our daily lives, such as utilizing your own bag rather than a single-use plastic bag.

By reusing, we ensure that we utilize an item more than once, for example, using a bag at least a few times before discarding it.

By recycling, we ensure that our product is reused rather than discarded.

2. Planting Trees.

HE WHO PLANTS A TREE PLANTS A HOPE

Planting trees and plants has numerous environmental benefits. It goes through photosynthesis, which reduces carbon dioxide in the air and releases oxygen. Thus, it decreases greenhouse gases, resulting in less global warming.

They also prevent soil erosion and help maintain cooler temperatures by providing shade. Trees and plants additionally provide food for us and the animals, ensuring the stability of the environment.

3. Educate Others.

TEACH, LOVE, INSPIRE

By informing and educating others on environmental issues and the risks and threats that our planet is currently facing, we can ensure that they participate in the conservation of the environment or even spread the message to others.

This enables more actions to be carried out, and as the phrase goes, 'two hands are better than one'. It protects the Earth from deteriorating and ensures that it can recover from human harmful actions.
