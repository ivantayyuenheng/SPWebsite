Singapore Polytechnic

Numerous schools have followed the Singapore Green Plan 2030 which advanced Singapore's main objective towards sustainable development, including Singapore Polytechnic. It brought pledges for sustainable development to progress and contribute many projects such as:

  • Green Mark Platinum Buildings
  • 110+ Water Dispensers
  • 13 Diplomas featuring Common Core Curriculum (CCC)

Not only that Singapore Polytechnic community also ensemble together to provide sustainable development contribution such as:

  • Researching making asphalt more durable using plastic pellets
  • Launches first solar photovoltaic recycling line

Singapore Polytechnic also won President's Award for the Environment in 2010 as they are recognized by many for their green award!

Singapore Polytechnic Contribution

Green Mark Platinum Buildings

New School of Business & AeroHub at Singapore Polytechnic located in around T18 area have acquired the required environmental impact and performance to achieve the green mark awards in 2015 .

Not only that, SP Sports Arena and T3B campus also recently achieved the Green Mark Platinum and Singapore Polytechnic have an aim to certify all 22 buildings in their school by 2025.

110+ Water Dispensers

More than 110 water dispensers could be found around the campus to encourage students to use it as a source of drinkable and clean water rather than using the alternative of buying plastic bottles.
This eliminates plastic bottles ending up in the environment and minimizes their production causing less harmful gases emitted.

13 Diploma featuring Common Core Curriculum

By educating and teaching students about global issues and its impact on our future, students will be increasingly aware of its dangers and learn different actions to keep the environment safe and healthy. It also allows students to create and develop their own prototype sustainability solutions which could be applied for real-life issues.

In addition, students are also able to tackle the issues in the UN SDGs (United Nations of Sustainable Development Goals) and create a sustainable future for Singapore.

1. Researching making asphalt more durable using plastic pellets

The use of plastic waste found in our day-to-day life was implemented in asphalt to build a higher durability of road surface for the first time ever in Singapore.

Samwoh, a local construction company, teamed up with researches and students in Singapore Polytechnic to discover that asphalts made from plastic waste had longer effective duration than regular asphalt by 20%.

Not only will this reduce plastic waste found in Singapore, but it also allow a useful implementation for it too.


2. Launches first solar photovoltaic recycling line

Singapore Polytechnic officially pilot their first solar photovoltaics in 1st November 2022. Controlled by a machine, it could retrieve and recycle precious materials - with a recovery rate of 95% - such as glass and silver by dismantling 80 solar panels a day.

This innovation also complement the existence of growing the local energy efficiency ecosystem.
